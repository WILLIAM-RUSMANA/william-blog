<h1>Target Scoping & Information Gathering</h1>

The course began with an explanation of a basic question that was raised: "So, what is a penetration test preparation really about?" Even before writing on a single tool a pentester had to set the scope for the engagement.

Target Scoping is basically the structured preliminary of any pentest: obtaining client requirements, composing test plans, identifying test boundaries, business objectives and defining a timeline. In other words: "it's like a check-list and a contract combined together in a single document that provides a level of safety for both parties". When gathering client information there is: company data, devices within scope (servers, workstations, networking devices, IDS/IPS, firewall), disaster recovery plan (if any), compliance, budget, timeframe and the point of contact name.

Penetration Testing Types was the introduction of terms that were repeated all through the course: black-box (external, without any previous knowledge by the pentester, simulates outsider attack), white-box (with previous knowledge of everything of a client, simulates insider attack), informed/uninformed (IT department has the previous notification or not), social engineering (imitating of employees or investigation of personal backgrounds in scope or not) and DoS testing (availability attacks).

Information Gathering is the first dynamic step of the penetration testing,Competitive intelligence means collecting and analyze information legally from public accessible sources-information of what can be observed, what an attacker could get etc. Introduced tools were Whois (information regarding IP address or domain), Host (DNS information), Paros (web proxy to understand web applications structure). The basics of HTTP protocol were introduced as well as the analysis of get requests and the headers of the server answer.

 Cookie files can also give information away (stored in user computer, used by web sites, store your preferences, etc.) as well as web bugs (invisible tracking on web applications). The practical exercise involved the OSINT of real Indonesian domains.
 
 Introduced Tool: Dradis- Reporting tool, used to documenting throughout pentest engagement.
