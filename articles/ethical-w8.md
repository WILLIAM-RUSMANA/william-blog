Social engineering is the manipulation of people rather than systems — and it is often the easiest path into a well-defended network.

Human Psychology is the foundation. Humans make decisions based on trust, authority, urgency, and social norms. A social engineer exploits these instincts rather than technical vulnerabilities.

The Attack Process:


Intelligence gathering — research the target and their organization
Identifying vulnerable points — who is susceptible, what channels work
Planning the attack — scripting the approach
Execution — carrying out the deception


Attack Methods:


Impersonation — pretending to be someone trusted (IT support, a vendor, a new employee)
Reciprocation — exchanging favors to build false trust
Influential Authority — leveraging perceived power (e.g., "I'm calling from the CEO's office")
Scarcity — creating urgency ("This needs to be fixed right now")
Social Relationship — exploiting existing familiarity


Social Engineering Toolkit (SET) — a Kali Linux tool for automating social engineering attacks. The practical demonstrated creating a fake Facebook login page to harvest credentials. SET creates a cloned site, serves it locally, and captures entered usernames and passwords — then redirects the victim to the real site so they don't notice.

CUPP (Common User Passwords Profiler) — generates custom wordlists based on a target's personal information (name, birthdate, partner, pets, company). Demonstrates why attackers research targets personally before attempting password attacks.
